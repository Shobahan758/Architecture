14+ Years of studio leadership
Principal Architect

Mira Collins

Mira leads concept strategy, residential architecture, and client presentations for high-detail private commissions.

Her work connects restrained planning with warm material choices, helping clients move from early sketches to buildable decisions with clarity.

Mira begins each commission by studying the daily rituals of the people who will use the space. That early research shapes room proportions, circulation, daylight, storage, and the quiet details that make a home feel considered rather than decorated.

Across residential and boutique hospitality projects, she coordinates closely with engineers, makers, and site teams so the design intent survives construction. Her process keeps drawings, samples, budgets, and client decisions moving in the same direction.

She is especially focused on tactile material palettes, calm spatial sequences, and long-life detailing. The result is architecture that feels precise on handover day and still natural years later.

During concept development, Mira prepares spatial options that compare atmosphere, cost, and construction impact side by side. Clients can understand not only how a proposal looks, but what each choice means for schedule, craft, and everyday use.

Her studio reviews are built around clear decisions. Plans are refined through working models, light studies, joinery sketches, and material boards, allowing the team to resolve questions before they become expensive site changes.

Mira also guides the presentation language for each project, from early mood direction to final handover documentation. This helps homeowners, consultants, and contractors stay aligned around one shared architectural intent.

Outside the drawing set, she mentors junior designers on proportion, restraint, and responsible specification. Her leadership keeps the studio practical, thoughtful, and deeply attentive to the people who will live with the work.
